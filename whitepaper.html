Chapter 01

The Problem
Space

Four structural failures in how AI, identity, and creative work intersect on the modern internet.

1.1 — AI without consent

AI systems are deployed against people without their knowledge or permission. Facial recognition identifies individuals in public spaces. Hiring algorithms screen candidates before a human reads their resume. Customer service bots process sensitive data under the guise of helpfulness.

The absence of consent is not accidental. It is structural. Consent is friction. Friction reduces conversion. Businesses optimise for conversion. The result is a systematic erosion of the right to be left alone.

"You should never be subjected to an AI system without your explicit permission."

— aiOut.me founding principle

1.2 — Builders without credibility

Developers who want to build AI responsibly have no way to prove it. There is no credential for ethical AI development. A builder can write a blog post or publish a policy document — but neither is verifiable, portable, or privacy-preserving.

Without a meaningful signal of ethical commitment, responsible builders are indistinguishable from irresponsible ones. The market cannot reward good behaviour it cannot see.

1.3 — Identity without privacy

Existing identity systems force a binary choice: full exposure or full anonymity. A developer who wants to publicly commit to ethical AI must either reveal their identity or remain silent. There is no mechanism for saying "I signed this pledge" without revealing who signed it.

Zero-knowledge proofs solve this mathematically. But they have not been applied to the problem of builder credentialing — until now.

1.4 — Creative work without provenance

Music, art, and video are shared millions of times per day. The creator of a track rarely knows where it has been played, by whom, or how many times. Royalty systems are opaque, slow, and controlled by platforms with conflicting interests.

NFTs promised to fix this but delivered speculation instead of provenance. The missing piece is not ownership — it is tracking. A living NFT that records every play and share on-chain, in real time.

Layer 1 — aiOut.me · Rights

Individuals add their name to a public append-only registry asserting their right to opt out of AI processing. The registry creates a documented record of consent refusal that can be cited in legal proceedings, regulatory submissions, and policy advocacy.

As AI regulation develops globally — the EU AI Act, proposed US federal frameworks, emerging APAC standards — a documented opt-out record provides standing for individuals and ammunition for advocates.

Layer 2 — keepAIon.com · Builders

Developers sign a public pledge committing to transparency, consent, and accountability. The pledge is not a restriction — it is a positive assertion of values. keepAIon occupies the credible centre between AI panic and AI recklessness.

"I commit to building AI tools with transparency, consent, and accountability. I keep AI on — and I build it right."

— keepAIon pledge

Layer 3 — AMOK · Zero-Knowledge Proof

AMOK (Autonomous Movement Of Knowledge) uses a Noir ZK circuit to generate a verifiable credential without revealing the holder's identity. The proof asserts: "I signed the keepAIon pledge" — without revealing who signed it.

// Circuit inputs
Private: secret, github_handle_hash
Public: commitment, nullifier, pledge_root
// Proof: knowledge of secret such that
// commitment ∈ pledge_tree ∧ nullifier = pedersen(secret, 0)

AMOK credentials require the holder to maintain a minimum Δ9 balance on Solana. This creates ongoing economic alignment — the credential stays active as long as the holder participates in the ecosystem economy. Soulbound ERC-721 on Base. Non-transferable.

Layer 4 — Δ9 · Economy

Solana SPL utility token providing economic incentives and governance across the stack. No burn mechanics — Δ9 is a participation signal. Holding is sufficient for base access. Governance weight proportional to holding duration.

Layer 5 — 4DNFT.com · Media

Traditional NFTs capture three dimensions of creative work: ownership, authenticity, scarcity. 4DNFT adds the fourth dimension — provenance over time. Every play and share recorded on-chain as an immutable event.

ERC-1155 on Base. ERC-2981 royalty splits wired in. Listener and sharer identities are hashed before recording — the chain knows a play happened, not who played it. Consent by design, consistent with the aiOut framework.

Chapter 03

Integration
Architecture

Flow A — Developer signs pledge

  1. 01Developer visits keepAIon.com, enters GitHub handle + passphrase
  2. 02AMOK generates ZKP in browser — private inputs never leave the device
  3. 03Commitment registered on Base — no identity revealed
  4. 04ZK proof submitted → AMOK soulbound token minted
  5. 05Developer holds Δ9 on Solana to keep credential active
  6. 06Badge embeds in GitHub README with verifiable on-chain link

Flow B — Artist mints offering

  1. 01Artist visits 4DNFT.com, connects wallet, uploads media to IPFS
  2. 02Calls mintOffering() — title, type, IPFS hash, royalty config
  3. 03If artist holds valid AMOK token → amokVerified = true in metadata
  4. 04Offering minted as ERC-1155 on Base
  5. 05Platform integrations begin recording plays and shares on-chain
  6. 06ERC-2981 royalties flow on every secondary sale

Flow C — Domain routing

  1. 01All five domains point to one hosted file on GoDaddy Aero
  2. 02index.html detects window.location.hostname on load
  3. 03aiout.me → red/black rights theme
  4. 04keepaion.com → green builder theme + AMOK pledge flow
  5. 05delta9.com → gold token theme
  6. 064dnft.com → purple media/artist theme
